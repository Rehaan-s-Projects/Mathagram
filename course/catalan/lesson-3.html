Unstressed Vowel Reduction

In Eastern Catalan (including Barcelona), unstressed vowels get reduced — they change to simpler, softer sounds. This is why spoken Catalan can sound different from how it's written.

Rule 1 — a / e → schwa [ə]

When a or e is unstressed, both become the neutral "uh" sound (schwa).

casa→càsə
pare→pàrə
Rule 2 — o → u [u]

When o is unstressed, it sounds like u.

botiga→butíga
mole→mule

Why It Matters

  • Reduction is the most distinctive feature of spoken Eastern Catalan.
  • Western Catalan (Valencia, Lleida) does NOT reduce — vowels sound closer to the spelling.
  • Writing stays the same — only pronunciation changes.
  • Listen carefully — a botiga (shop) sounds like "bu-TI-ga"!
