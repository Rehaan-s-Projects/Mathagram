Unit 39 — Geometric (Clifford) Algebra

From Cross Product to Geometric Product

The cross product is awkward: it only works in 3D, and it conflates "an oriented area" with "a vector perpendicular to it." Geometric algebra (Clifford algebra) generalizes by introducing the geometric product:

$$\vec{a}\vec{b} = \vec{a}\cdot\vec{b} + \vec{a}\wedge\vec{b}$$

It's the sum of a scalar (the dot product) and a bivector (the wedge — an oriented plane element). One product encoding both pieces of geometric information.
