Unit 39 — Geometric (Clifford) Algebra

The Clifford Algebra Cl(n)

The Clifford algebra \(\text{Cl}(n)\) over \(\mathbb{R}^n\) is generated by basis vectors \(\vec{e}_1, \ldots, \vec{e}_n\) satisfying

$$\vec{e}_i^2 = 1, \quad \vec{e}_i \vec{e}_j = -\vec{e}_j \vec{e}_i \ (i \neq j)$$

So vectors square to scalars, and distinct basis vectors anti-commute. The algebra contains scalars, vectors, bivectors, ..., up to the top-grade pseudoscalar. Total dimension: \(2^n\).

For Cl(1, 3) (Minkowski signature), \(\vec{e}_0^2 = 1\) but \(\vec{e}_i^2 = -1\) for \(i = 1, 2, 3\). This is the spacetime algebra.
