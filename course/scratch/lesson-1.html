A Block-Based Programming Language

Code is just a sequence of instructions. Drag the pseudocode blocks below into the code area to send the cat to the fish — then press Run to execute your program, just like dragging blocks in Scratch.

Hi, I'm Diego! I'll be your guide. Block-based coding is just stacking instructions in order. Drag a block from the palette into your program, snap them into a sequence, and press Run to watch the cat follow your steps. Help her catch the fish — and once you do, see if you can get there with fewer blocks using Repeat.
Challenge: Guide the cat (🐱) to the fish (🐟). The cat starts facing up. Use as few blocks as you can.
