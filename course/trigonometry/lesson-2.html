Angle Measurement and Types

Angles are classified by their size. Understanding these categories is essential for working with triangles and geometric proofs throughout trigonometry.

  • Acute angle: Greater than 0° but less than 90°
  • Right angle: Exactly 90°
  • Obtuse angle: Greater than 90° but less than 180°
  • Straight angle: Exactly 180°
  • Reflex angle: Greater than 180° but less than 360°

Two important angle relationships come up constantly in trigonometry:

  • Complementary angles: Two angles that add up to 90°. For example, 35° and 55° are complementary.
  • Supplementary angles: Two angles that add up to 180°. For example, 60° and 120° are supplementary.

A quick way to remember: "c" comes before "s" in the alphabet, and 90 comes before 180 — so complementary = 90° and supplementary = 180°.
