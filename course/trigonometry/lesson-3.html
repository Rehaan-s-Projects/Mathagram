Unit 3 — Lesson 9

SOH-CAH-TOA

The three primary trigonometric ratios — sine, cosine, and tangent — relate the sides of a right triangle to its angles. The mnemonic SOH-CAH-TOA is the easiest way to remember them:

  • SOH — \(\sin(\theta) = \dfrac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)
  • CAH — \(\cos(\theta) = \dfrac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)
  • TOA — \(\tan(\theta) = \dfrac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}}\)

The hypotenuse is always the longest side of a right triangle — the one opposite the right angle. The opposite and adjacent sides depend on which angle \(\theta\) you are looking at.

Example: Consider a classic 3-4-5 right triangle where the side opposite \(\theta\) is 3 and the side adjacent to \(\theta\) is 4:

$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{3}{5}, \quad \cos(\theta) = \frac{4}{5}, \quad \tan(\theta) = \frac{3}{4}$$

A useful identity to know: \(\tan(\theta) = \dfrac{\sin(\theta)}{\cos(\theta)}\). This follows directly from dividing the SOH and CAH definitions.
