Types of Triangles

Triangles are the fundamental shape of trigonometry. Before diving into trig ratios, you need to know how triangles are classified — both by their sides and by their angles.

Classified by sides:

  • Equilateral — All three sides are equal in length. All three angles are 60°.
  • Isosceles — Exactly two sides are equal. The two base angles opposite the equal sides are also equal.
  • Scalene — All three sides have different lengths, and all three angles are different.

Classified by angles:

  • Acute triangle — All three angles are less than 90°.
  • Right triangle — One angle is exactly 90°. This is the triangle used in SOH-CAH-TOA.
  • Obtuse triangle — One angle is greater than 90°.

One of the most important facts in geometry: the angles of any triangle always add up to 180°. This means a triangle can never have two right angles (that would already total 180°, leaving nothing for the third angle).

$$\alpha + \beta + \gamma = 180°$$
