Unit 1 — Alphabet & Sounds

Vowel Sounds — Long Vowels

Frisian long vowels are held longer than short vowels. They are often marked with a circumflex (^) accent.

  • aa / â — like "a" in "father" (e.g., skaap = sheep)
  • ee / ê — like "ay" in "say" (e.g., trêe = step)
  • ii / î — like "ee" in "see" (e.g., tiid = time)
  • oo / ô — like "o" in "go" (e.g., boat = boat)
  • uu / û — like "oo" in "moon" (e.g., hûs = house)

The circumflex accent (^) is a distinctive feature of Frisian spelling.
