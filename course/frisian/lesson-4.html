Diphthongs in Frisian

A diphthong is two vowel sounds combined into one syllable. Frisian is rich in diphthongs — more than Dutch or English!

  • ei — like "ay" in "day" (e.g., dei = day)
  • ai — like "eye" (e.g., aai = egg)
  • au/ou — like "ow" in "how" (e.g., blau = blue)
  • oe — like "oo" in "food" (e.g., boek = book)
  • ie — like "ee" in "beer" (e.g., bier = beer)
  • oa — no English equivalent, between "o" and "a"

Frisian has over 20 diphthongs — one of the most of any Germanic language!
