Unit 1 — Checkers (Draughts)

King Me! (Promotion)

When a regular piece reaches the opposite end of the board (the last row), it gets promoted to a King. A second checker is stacked on top to mark it.

  • Kings can move and capture both forward and backward.
  • This makes Kings much more powerful than regular pieces.
  • In tournament rules, the turn ends when a piece is kinged — even if another jump is available.
  • Protecting your back row delays your opponent from getting Kings.

Strategy tip: Try to get Kings early while preventing your opponent from doing the same!
