Unit 1 — Checkers (Draughts)

Jumping & Capturing

In checkers, you capture an opponent's piece by jumping over it diagonally. Your piece lands on the empty square beyond the captured piece, and the captured piece is removed from the board.

Black pieces capture by jumping diagonally forward over a red piece onto an empty square.

  • You can only jump if the square beyond is empty.
  • The jumped piece is removed from the board immediately.
  • Regular pieces can only capture forward (diagonally).
  • If a capture is available, you must take it (forced capture rule).

Example: A black piece on square 10 can jump over a red piece on square 14 to land on square 17 — removing the red piece.
