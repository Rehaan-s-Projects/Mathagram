Unit 1 — Checkers (Draughts)

Multiple Jumps

If after making one jump your piece lands next to another opponent's piece with an empty square beyond, you must continue jumping. This is called a multiple jump or double/triple jump.

  • All jumps in a sequence are part of one turn.
  • You remove each captured piece as you jump over it.
  • You must keep jumping as long as captures are available.
  • The direction can change with each jump — you can zig-zag!

Example: Black jumps over red on square 14, lands on 18, then immediately jumps another red on 22, landing on 25 — capturing two pieces in one turn!
