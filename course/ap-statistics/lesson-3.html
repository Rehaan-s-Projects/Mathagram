Measures of Center

Finding the "Middle" of Data

Measures of center describe where the "middle" or "typical" value of a data set lies. The three main measures are mean, median, and mode.

Mean (Average)

The mean is the sum of all values divided by the number of values:

$$\bar{x} = \frac{\sum x_i}{n}$$

Median

The median is the middle value when the data is sorted from smallest to largest. If there's an even number of values, the median is the average of the two middle values.

Mode

The mode is the value that appears most frequently. A data set can have no mode, one mode, or multiple modes.

Example

Data: 2, 3, 3, 5, 7

  • Mean = (2 + 3 + 3 + 5 + 7) / 5 = 20 / 5 = 4
  • Median = 3 (the middle value)
  • Mode = 3 (appears twice)
Key Insight: The mean is pulled toward outliers (extreme values), while the median is resistant — it stays stable even when outliers are present. This is why median home price is often more useful than mean home price.
