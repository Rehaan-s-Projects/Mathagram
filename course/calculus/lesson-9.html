Unit 2 — Differentiation Basics

Power, Constant, and Sum Rules

Using the limit definition every single time would be exhausting. Thankfully, there are shortcut rules that let you differentiate most functions instantly.

Power Rule: Bring the exponent down and subtract one.

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[x^n\right] = n x^{n-1}$$

Constant Rule: The derivative of any constant is zero.

$$\frac{d}{dx}[c] = 0$$

Constant Multiple Rule: Constants slide out of the derivative.

$$\frac{d}{dx}[c \cdot f(x)] = c \cdot f'(x)$$

Sum Rule: Differentiate term by term.

$$\frac{d}{dx}[f(x) + g(x)] = f'(x) + g'(x)$$

Example: Differentiate \(f(x) = 4x^3 - 2x^2 + x\).

$$f'(x) = 12x^2 - 4x + 1$$

Each term is handled independently: bring the exponent down, subtract one from it, and keep the constant multiplier. That's the whole game.
