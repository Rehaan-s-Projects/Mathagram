Unit 1 — Limits

Limits Involving Trig Identities

Limits of trig functions are often "\(0/0\)" and can't be evaluated by direct substitution. Two identities let you unlock almost all of them:

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1 \qquad\qquad \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x} = 0$$

Both apply when \(x\) is measured in radians. Together with the algebraic limit laws and trig identities, they cover most classic trig limits.

Example 1. Evaluate \(\lim_{x\to 0}\dfrac{\sin 5x}{x}\).

Rewrite: \(\dfrac{\sin 5x}{x} = 5 \cdot \dfrac{\sin 5x}{5x}\). As \(x \to 0\), \(5x \to 0\), so \(\dfrac{\sin 5x}{5x} \to 1\). The limit is \(5 \cdot 1 = 5\).

General rule. \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0}\dfrac{\sin(kx)}{x} = k\) for any constant \(k\).

Example 2. Evaluate \(\lim_{x\to 0}\dfrac{\sin 4x}{\sin 3x}\).

Split: \(\dfrac{\sin 4x}{\sin 3x} = \dfrac{\sin 4x}{4x} \cdot \dfrac{3x}{\sin 3x} \cdot \dfrac{4}{3}\). The first factor \(\to 1\), the second \(\to 1\), and the third is constant, so the limit is \(\tfrac{4}{3}\).

Example 3. Evaluate \(\lim_{x\to 0}\dfrac{1-\cos x}{x^2}\).

Multiply top and bottom by \(1 + \cos x\):

$$\frac{1-\cos x}{x^2}\cdot\frac{1+\cos x}{1+\cos x} = \frac{\sin^2 x}{x^2(1+\cos x)} \to 1^2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{2}.$$

Example 4. Evaluate \(\lim_{x\to 0}\dfrac{\tan x}{x}\). Write \(\tan x = \dfrac{\sin x}{\cos x}\), so \(\dfrac{\tan x}{x} = \dfrac{\sin x}{x}\cdot\dfrac{1}{\cos x} \to 1 \cdot 1 = 1\).
