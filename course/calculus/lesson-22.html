Unit 2 — Derivatives

Power Rule & Basic Rules

Using the limit definition every time would be painfully slow. Thankfully, there are shortcut rules that let you find derivatives quickly. The most important one is the Power Rule.

The Power Rule: If \(f(x) = x^n\), where \(n\) is any real number, then:

$$\frac{d}{dx}\left[x^n\right] = n \cdot x^{n-1}$$

Bring the exponent down as a coefficient, then subtract one from the exponent.

The Constant Rule: The derivative of a constant is zero.

$$\frac{d}{dx}[c] = 0$$

The Constant Multiple Rule: Constants factor out of derivatives.

$$\frac{d}{dx}[c \cdot f(x)] = c \cdot f'(x)$$

The Sum/Difference Rule: The derivative of a sum (or difference) is the sum (or difference) of the derivatives.

$$\frac{d}{dx}[f(x) \pm g(x)] = f'(x) \pm g'(x)$$

Example 1: Find the derivative of \(f(x) = 4x^5\).

$$f'(x) = 4 \cdot 5x^4 = 20x^4$$

Example 2: Find the derivative of \(f(x) = 3x^4 - 2x^3 + 7x - 9\).

$$f'(x) = 12x^3 - 6x^2 + 7$$

Example 3: Find the derivative of \(f(x) = \frac{1}{x^2} = x^{-2}\).

$$f'(x) = -2x^{-3} = -\frac{2}{x^3}$$

Example 4: Find the derivative of \(f(x) = \sqrt{x} = x^{1/2}\).

$$f'(x) = \frac{1}{2}x^{-1/2} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}$$
