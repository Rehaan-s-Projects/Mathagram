Unit 1 — Limits

Limits at Removable Discontinuities with Trig

Two special trigonometric limits show up constantly in calculus. You need to memorize both of them:

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1$$
$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x} = 0$$

These are both \(\frac{0}{0}\) indeterminate forms, but the limits exist. The first one says that \(\sin x\) and \(x\) behave almost identically near zero. The second says that \(1 - \cos x\) shrinks to zero faster than \(x\) does.

Example 1:

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(3x)}{x}$$

Rewrite to match the known form: multiply and divide by 3.

$$= \lim_{x \to 0} 3 \cdot \frac{\sin(3x)}{3x} = 3 \cdot 1 = 3$$

Example 2:

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(5x)}{\sin(2x)}$$

Rewrite each sine using the known limit:

$$= \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(5x)}{5x} \cdot \frac{2x}{\sin(2x)} \cdot \frac{5x}{2x} = 1 \cdot 1 \cdot \frac{5}{2} = \frac{5}{2}$$

The general pattern: \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin(ax)}{bx} = \frac{a}{b}\).
